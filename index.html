Introduction

Hypoxic ischemic encephalopathy (HIE) is a brain injury that occurs in 1 âˆ¼ 5/1000 term-born neonates. HIE lesion detection is a crucial step in clinical care of HIE. It could lead to a more accurate estimation of prognosis, a better understanding of neurological symptoms, and a timely prediction of response to therapy in this population. The rise of Artificial Intelligence (AI) brings hope to objectively and accurately finding HIE lesions. In order to bring together researchers interested in HIE Lesion Detection and faciliate research in this area, we host two benchmark challenges on HIE Lesion Detection and Outcome Prediction. The workshop consists of presentations by clinical and machine learning experts in the field and short talks regarding methods addressing the benchmark challenges in this disease. This is the first workshop on HIE Lesion Detection and Outcome Prediction. Challenges

We establish two challenges:

  • HIE Lesion Detection: to predict a binary mask of HIE lesion.
  • HIE Outcome Prediction: to prediction HIE outcome.
